The Microplastics Problem

Made by: Manente Davide - Melana Paolo - Fernando Shavindu

What Are Microplastics?

Microplastics are tiny plastic particles less than 5 millimeters in size. They are formed through the breakdown of larger plastic items, such as bottles, bags, and fishing nets, due to environmental exposure and degradation processes like UV radiation and mechanical wear. Microplastics can also originate from products like cosmetics, synthetic clothing, and industrial processes.

Microplastics and Marine Life

Ingestion and Digestive Problems: Marine animals often mistake microplastics for food, leading to blockages and internal injuries.

Toxicity: Microplastics absorb pollutants such as pesticides and heavy metals, which then enter the food chain.

Reproductive System Disruption: Chemicals in plastics can interfere with reproductive systems of marine species.

Ecosystem Unbalance: Disruption at the plankton level impacts the entire marine food web.

Bioaccumulation: Microplastics accumulate in higher predators and potentially reach humans via seafood.

Conclusion: Microplastics threaten marine life through physical harm, toxicity, and food chain disruptions.

Microplastics and Human Health: An Emerging Concern

Microplastics have been detected in human blood, placenta, and even the brain. Their ability to circulate within the body raises concerns about long-term health effects, such as inflammation, oxidative stress, and hormonal disruption. Although studies are ongoing, the urgency for regulation and awareness is growing.

Health Effects by Exposure Level

Low-Level Exposure

  • Mild skin changes like dryness and irritation
  • Gut microbiome imbalances leading to bloating and discomfort
  • Fatigue due to low-level inflammation

Mid-Level Exposure

  • Skin irritation and premature aging
  • Red, irritated eyes from air pollution
  • Coughing and wheezing
  • Hormonal imbalances and digestive issues
  • Brain fog and persistent fatigue

High-Level Exposure

  • Chronic skin issues and inflammation
  • Premature aging signs
  • Discolored lips/fingers from poor lung function
  • Neurological symptoms (slow reflexes, confusion)
  • Hormonal and reproductive issues
  • Hair thinning, skin lesions
